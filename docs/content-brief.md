# Content Brief & Sitemap
## AI Marketing & Brand Strategy Platform for International Investors Entering Syria

**Prepared for:** Website build in Claude Code
**Positioning:** Bilingual (Arabic/English) AI-powered marketing and communications consultancy specializing in Syria's reconstruction economy, serving international investors from any origin market (Gulf, Turkey, Europe, North America, diaspora capital).

---

## 1. Strategic Foundation

### 1.1 Core positioning statement
The platform is the flagship voice for **marketing, brand, and communications strategy for companies investing in Syria's reconstruction economy** — not a generalist Gulf marketing agency, and not limited to any single investor nationality. The differentiator is depth on one specific, timely, underserved market moment, delivered bilingually with real Arabic craft (not translated English).

### 1.2 Why now
- Western sanctions on Syria were substantially unwound in 2025 (US embargo revoked in June 2025, EU lifting most restrictions in May 2025), opening a genuine reconstruction economy.
- Gulf capital is already committing at scale (e.g., Saudi pledges toward tourism, medical, telecom, and entertainment sectors).
- Reliable market data, established local brand trust, and structured digital marketing infrastructure are all scarce in Syria right now — this is the gap the platform is built to fill.
- Buyers are early, sophisticated, and risk-aware — content and tools need to read as rigorous and evidence-based, not promotional.

### 1.3 Who the site is for
Segment by **shared problem**, not by investor nationality:
1. **Energy & infrastructure investors** — need stakeholder and government-facing communications, technical credibility content.
2. **Telecom & tech entrants** — need consumer-facing brand entry, digital-first campaigns.
3. **Real estate & tourism developers** — need aspirational brand-building, diaspora engagement, visual storytelling.
4. **Healthcare operators** — need trust-heavy, community-facing communications.
5. **Agriculture & agribusiness** — need practical, localized messaging and cooperative/community relationships.

Every investor in every sector shares four problems the site should speak to directly: **data scarcity, reputational scrutiny, trust-building from zero, and multi-audience communication** (Syrian consumers/partners in Arabic, international stakeholders in English, government/community bodies in both).

### 1.4 Tone & brand voice
- Rigorous, evidence-first, quietly confident — never hype-driven ("AI-powered growth hacks" energy is off-brand).
- Comfortable naming risk and scrutiny openly (this builds trust faster than avoiding the topic).
- Bilingual as craft, not translation — every core page and blog pillar should be written natively in both languages, not machine-translated.

## 3. Page-by-Page Content Briefs

### 3.1 Home (`/`)
**Goal:** Convert a cold, skeptical, risk-aware visitor into a booked call or a scorecard completion within 60 seconds of scanning.

**Structure:**
1. **Hero** — one-sentence positioning statement + one credibility line (data point, not adjective). Primary CTA: "Get your Syria Market-Entry Readiness Score." Secondary CTA: "Book a call."
2. **The moment, stated plainly** — 2–3 sentences on why Syria's reconstruction economy is investable now, cited to real figures (sanctions timeline, pledged capital).
3. **The four shared problems** (data scarcity, scrutiny, trust-from-zero, multi-audience communication) as a scannable 4-card grid — this is the empathy section that proves you understand the buyer's actual situation.
4. **Flagship tool embed or strong CTA card** — Readiness Scorecard.
5. **Sector strip** — 5 sector cards linking to `/sectors/*`.
6. **Capability proof** — logos/certifications if available; otherwise a "how we work" 3-step methodology strip (audit → strategy → execution) standing in for case studies.
7. **Latest insights** — 3 most recent posts, mixed AR/EN.
8. **Closing CTA band** — Book a call + WhatsApp button.

### 3.2 About / Methodology (`/about`)
- Founder narrative: marble/stone B2B export background, MENA market fluency, PhD research in geoeconomics/de-dollarization as evidence of genuine regional and macro expertise (this is a real, distinctive credibility asset — use it).
- Explicit methodology walkthrough: how an AI marketing readiness audit actually works, how AI tools are evaluated before being recommended to a client (transparency as differentiator).
- Language/craft statement: what "genuinely bilingual" means in practice (native drafting, not translation).

### 3.3 Services (`/services`)
Four core service lines, each with its own page (problem → approach → deliverable → who it's for):
1. **Market-Entry Strategy** — data-scarce market research, proxy-data modeling, primary research design.
2. **Responsible Investment Communications** — transparent reconstruction narratives; stakeholder communications built for scrutiny, not spin.
3. **AI Marketing Execution** — campaign build, Arabic-first content, AI-assisted personalization, GEO/AI-answer-engine optimization.
4. **Diaspora & Community Bridge Strategy** — leveraging Syrian diaspora networks for trust, talent, and local credibility.

Consider **tiered packaging** on each service page: workshop/audit (SME), project-based strategy engagement (mid-size), retainer/advisory (large corporate or reconstruction-linked entity).

### 3.4 Sectors (`/sectors/*`)
Each sector page follows the same template: sector context (2–3 sentences) → what this sector specifically needs from marketing/comms (pulled from the table in §1.3) → relevant service links → relevant tool (e.g., cost-of-inaction calculator preset to sector benchmarks).

### 3.5 Tools (`/tools`)
See §4 for full specs. Tools hub page briefly frames all four tools as "evidence-based, not hype" and links out.

### 3.6 Insights / Blog (`/insights`)
Bilingual blog with a language filter. See §5 for the full content pillar system.

### 3.7 Book a Call (`/book-a-call`)
Calendly-style embedded booking widget, no long-form contact form in front of it — reduce friction. Short pre-call qualifying questions (sector, investment stage, primary market of origin) to route to the right consultant conversation.

### 3.8 Contact (`/contact`)
WhatsApp Business button as the primary channel (outperforms email for first contact across Gulf/Levant B2B), with a simple form as fallback.

## 4. Interactive Tools — Functional Specs

These are lead-generation instruments as much as they are utility — each should end in an email-gated results screen and a "book a call to discuss your score" CTA.

### 4.1 Syria Market-Entry Readiness Scorecard *(flagship — homepage centerpiece)*
- **Inputs:** sector, target investment budget tier, risk tolerance, prior MENA experience (y/n), primary market of origin.
- **Output:** a 0–100 readiness score across sub-dimensions (data readiness, stakeholder-communication readiness, local-trust readiness, brand localization readiness) + flagged risks in plain language.
- **Why it works:** it's the single clearest expression of what the consultancy does, and it's a category no competitor has built.

### 4.2 AI Marketing ROI Calculator
- **Inputs:** current marketing spend, team size, current response/conversion times.
- **Output:** estimated efficiency/revenue gain, benchmarked against cited published figures (footnote every assumption — credibility depends on this being auditable, not a black box).

### 4.3 Cost of Inaction Calculator
- **Inputs:** same base inputs as ROI calculator, sector selector.
- **Output:** quantified estimate of value likely being lost by delaying AI-marketing adoption, using cited public multipliers. Loss-aversion framing, kept strictly evidence-based.

### 4.4 Campaign Roadmap Simulator
- **Inputs:** sector, market (Syria vs. a comparison market), budget tier.
- **Output:** a simulated campaign roadmap/timeline output — this doubles as a live demonstration of the consultant's actual thinking before a call is ever booked.

**Build note for Claude Code:** these can share one React component shell (multi-step form → results screen → gated CTA) with per-tool config objects for inputs/scoring logic, rather than four fully separate builds.

| **Answer-engine-friendly explainers** | SEO/GEO fuel; directly useful | Direct-answer FAQ posts on real search queries ("Is AI marketing worth it for an international investor entering Syria?") |

**Editorial rules:**
- Publish natively in both languages — never a translation afterthought.
- Lead every post with a complete, direct answer in the first 1–2 paragraphs (best for mobile skimming *and* for getting cited by AI answer engines).
- React to real current events within the pillars (sanctions developments, reconstruction investment announcements, Vision 2030 milestones) — timely posts earn more attention and citations than evergreen-only content.
- Cadence: one strong post every 1–2 weeks, sustained, beats a burst-then-silence pattern.

**Flagship recurring asset:** a quarterly *"State of AI Marketing: Gulf & Levant"* report, built from public data plus (as it accumulates) proprietary client data — gated as a lead magnet, positioned to become the thing journalists and AI answer engines cite.

## 6. Design & Localization Requirements

- **True bilingual, true RTL** — mirrored navigation, right-aligned text, RTL-aware iconography for Arabic. Not a translated-string overlay on an LTR template.
- **Arabic web fonts built for screens:** Tajawal, Cairo, IBM Plex Sans Arabic, or Almarai.
- **Mobile-first build** — mobile accounts for the large majority of relevant regional traffic; a desktop-first build will structurally underperform.
- **Visual tone:** premium and restrained — deep navy, emerald, or warm neutrals with gold accents to signal trust and seriousness; avoid generic "tech blue" SaaS styling.
- **Trust signals prominent:** credentials, methodology transparency, and (as available) client logos/certifications placed high on the page — MENA B2B buyers weigh this heavily before booking a call with an unfamiliar consultant.

## 7. Technical Notes for Claude Code Build

- **Recommended stack:** static/SSR site (e.g., Next.js) for SEO/GEO performance, deployed on Railway.
- **Environments:** use Railway's project/environment model — one project for the whole app, with a `staging` environment to test the Arabic RTL layout and full content before promoting to `production`. Each environment carries its own variables and is network-isolated from the others.
- **i18n:** structure content as language-keyed data (not hardcoded per-page duplication) so `/ar/...` routes and the language toggle stay in sync as blog content grows.
- **Tools architecture:** build the four interactive tools (§4) as a shared multi-step-form component with per-tool config, to avoid four divergent codebases.
- **Lead capture:** every tool result screen and the quarterly report should gate behind a simple email-capture step feeding into a shared leads store (CRM-ready).
